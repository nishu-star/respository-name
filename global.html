Promote Sustainable Consumption of Resources

Encouraging energy conservation and reducing waste for a sustainable future.

SDG 12

Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns essential for life. While populations continue to increase, the global resource supply diminishes We need to reform our per capita consumption(the yearly use of goods and services by each person), mostly when transitioning from unsustainable energy to reducing overall usage.

Renewable Resources

Renewable energy comes from sources that are naturally replenished on a human timescale. These include: Solar Energy, Wind Energy, Hydropower, Biomass, and Geothermal Energy Environmentally friendly with low greenhouse gas emissions, Sustainable and abundant, Reduces dependence on fossil fuels.

Non-Renewable Resources

Non-renewable energy comes from sources that do not replenish quickly and are finite. These include: Coal, Oil, Natural Gas, and Nuclear Energy Limited availability, and High greenhouse gas emissions

Energy conservation and waste reduction are vital to achieving SDG 12

  • Resource Efficiency: - we use natural resources more efficiently, reducing demand for non-renewable resources.
  • Reduced Emissions - Energy conservation leads to lower greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Economic Savings - Reducing energy consumption can lead to cost savings, promoting economic growth.
  • Minimized Environmental Impact - Reducing waste decreases the amount of waste in landfills and oceans, reducing pollution and protecting natural habitats.
  • Resource Recovery - Waste reduction involves recycling and composting, which allows valuable materials to be recovered and reused, reducing the need for new materials.
  • Health and Well-being - Proper waste management reduces the risk of health issues.

    • By promoting energy conservation and waste reduction, we can make significant strides toward achieving SDG 12 and creating a sustainable future. These are crucial for SDG 12's target to substantially reduce waste generation, achieve sustainable management and efficient use of natural resources, and reduce the adverse environmental impacts of consumption and production

Energy Conservation

  • Use renewable energy sources over non-renewable ones.
  • Adopt energy-efficient practices, such as using LED bulbs and appliances.
  • Improve home insulation and use programmable thermostats.
  • Promote electric vehicles and public transportation.

Reducing Waste

  • Recycle and compost to reduce landfill waste.
  • Get the correct amount of groceries, so you don't throw the left out ones, or the expired ones.
  • Use reusable products to minimize single-use plastics.
  • Reduce food waste through meal planning and proper food storage.
  • Educate consumers about the importance of reducing waste.
